# CERN access

## Registration categories

Start from the CERN Users Office. It provides support services to CERN Users, Cooperation Associates, and Visiting Scientists, and points other external categories to the appropriate pre-registration flows.

Common student cases are handled through an institute, team leader, supervisor, or experiment secretariat. Your exact category matters because it controls contract paperwork, site access, computing eligibility, insurance questions, and administrative responsibilities.

## Accounts and identity

CERN's authentication service provides centralized authentication and authorization. Its documentation lists the main services around Single Sign-On, the Users Portal, group management, application registration, resource subscriptions, and certificate services.

In practical terms, your CERN identity is the basis for:

- Signing in to CERN web services.
- Joining groups and mailing lists.
- Requesting or receiving access to applications.
- Using collaboration services such as CERNBox, GitLab, Indico, and Mattermost.
- Accessing computing services when your role is eligible.

## Site access and safety

Physical access depends on your registration status, location, safety training, and the areas you need to enter. Do not assume that a CERN account is enough for site or experimental-area access.

Before coming on site, ask your supervisor:

- Which CERN site or building you need.
- Whether you need a CERN access card.
- Which safety courses are mandatory.
- Whether your work involves radiation areas, dosimetry, laboratories, workshops, cryogenics, electrical work, or underground access.

## Life at CERN

The Users Office maintains practical information about first-day onboarding, life at CERN, life in the local area, health insurance and social security for associated members of personnel, Swiss and French residence permits, and contractual changes.

For non-technical questions, use:

- Users Office pages for registration and life-at-CERN topics.
- CERN Service Portal for service requests and incidents.
- Your institute or supervisor for contract, travel, and funding questions.
