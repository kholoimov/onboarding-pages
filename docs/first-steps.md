# First steps

## Before arrival

1. Confirm your CERN status with your supervisor or team leader. The Users Office supports CERN Users, Cooperation Associates, and Visiting Scientists, and its pre-arrival page distinguishes these categories from other external participant registrations.
2. Complete the registration path given by your institute, team leader, or CERN contact. Keep copies of institute letters, identity documents, and contract or invitation details ready.
3. Ask which SHiP groups you should join. At minimum, you usually need the relevant mailing list or group membership before private links, meetings, and repositories become convenient.
4. Read the public SHiP overview so you can place your project in the experiment context.
5. Ask your supervisor what your first technical task is: documentation, simulation, reconstruction, geometry, analysis, packaging, or operations.

## First day

The CERN Users Office has a dedicated first-day and onboarding section covering what happens after pre-registration, what to do in the first week, and how the first two months usually settle.

Use the first day to verify:

- You can sign in with your CERN account.
- Your CERN profile and affiliation look correct in the Users Portal.
- You can access the SHiP meetings, documents, and communication channels you need.
- You know who approves access requests for your group.
- You know whether your work needs safety training, site access, radiation-zone access, or computing-group membership.

## First week

Set up the daily tools:

- CERN Single Sign-On and two-factor authentication.
- CERNBox for documents and shared files.
- GitLab for code and issue tracking.
- Indico for meetings and agendas.
- Mattermost or mailing lists for day-to-day communication.
- LXPLUS, HTCondor, CVMFS, EOS, or experiment-specific environments if your work involves computing.

## First month

By the end of the first month, you should have:

- A short written description of your project.
- A working local or CERN-hosted development environment.
- Access to the repositories and datasets you need.
- A regular meeting rhythm with your supervisor or working group.
- A known path for help: local supervisor, SHiP contact, CERN Service Portal, or Users Office.
