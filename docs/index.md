# SHiP Student Onboarding

This guide is for students starting work with the [SHiP experiment](https://ship.web.cern.ch/) and the CERN environment. It collects the first practical steps, the CERN services you are likely to use, and the SHiP-specific places where collaboration work usually happens.

!!! note
    CERN access depends on your registration category, institute, role, and experiment membership. Treat this page as a practical map, then follow the official CERN and SHiP links for binding instructions.

## What SHiP is

SHiP, the Search for Hidden Particles experiment, is a fixed-target experiment in preparation at CERN's SPS. The public SHiP site describes it as a general-purpose experiment at the High-Intensity ECN3 Facility searching for hidden particles, including heavy neutral leptons, dark photons, dark scalars, axion-like particles, light dark matter candidates, and tau-neutrino interactions.

## What becomes available through CERN association

After the relevant CERN registration and account steps, students commonly gain access to a CERN identity, Single Sign-On, the Users Portal, group and mailing-list membership, CERNBox storage, GitLab, Indico meetings, Mattermost chat, computing resources such as LXPLUS and batch where eligible, site access where authorized, and support through the CERN Service Portal.

Not every service is automatic. Some resources are attached to experiment membership, computing-group membership, safety course completion, supervisor approval, or a specific request.
